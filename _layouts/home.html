Gender Equality

WiCS aims to bring together women and men who support the advancement of womxn in computer science in academia and in industry.

Support for New Majors

We support and encourage prospective computer science majors and provide direction and assistance to current computer science students.

Corporate

We are able to do what we do with the help of our generous sponsors.

  • Organize a Site Visit

    We organize tours, tech talks and (of course) lunches at the offices of the best companies in Manhattan. If your company would like to set up a site visit with us,
    send us a note.

  • Co-host an event with WICS

    We welcome any collaborative event, from tech talks to study breaks! If you would like to sponsor an event on Columbia's campus to connect students with opportunities at your company, raise awareness about the cool things you're doing, hold mock interview sessions or just have coffee and cupcakes with us, let us know.

  • Attend our Networking Event

    Every year in early April, we host a networking dinner for Columbia women in computer science, alumni, and corporate guests. We would be happy to have you too, whether as a sponsor or an invited guest!

  • Sponsor WiCS

    WiCS hosts a number of academic and social events throughout the school year, including lunches with faculty, study breaks, and mentorship activities. We also try to send as many students as possible to the Grace Hopper Conference. If you are interested in sponsoring one of these events or providing scholarships to Grace Hopper, definitely get in touch!

  • Open Job Positions

    Do you have a job you would like to broadcast to our members? Please visit our LinkedIn group and post the job as a discussion.

Meet the WiCS Board 2019-20! *************************************************** Haley So is a rising senior majoring in Electrical Engineering and minoring in Computer Science in SEAS class of 2020. This year, she is the President of WiCS and looks forward to supporting the incredible community of womxn in tech on campus. Last year as Vice President, she started the WiCS Event Coordinator Program, guiding a group of over 25 in planning initiatives and creating community. And previously as Diversity Chair, she co-led DivHacks, Columbia's first diversity hackathon. At the moment, she's pursuing her interests in photonics, quantum computing, and data science. Outside of class, she enjoys painting, ballet, exploring the city, and of course, creating fun gizmos in her garage and learning more computer science.
